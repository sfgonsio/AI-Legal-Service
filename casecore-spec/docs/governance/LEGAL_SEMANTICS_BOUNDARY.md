# LEGAL SEMANTICS BOUNDARY

## Purpose
Define the language boundaries that prevent overstatement.

## Distinctions
### Supported vs Proven
Supported means backed by evidence within system records.
Proven is a legal/adjudicative conclusion and must not be casually implied.

### Coverage vs Sufficiency
Coverage means evidence has been mapped to an element.
Sufficiency is a legal judgment and must remain attorney-controlled.

### Proposal vs Canonical
Proposal is suggested, untrusted, or pending review.
Canonical is platform-governed and approved for system-of-record use.

### Conflict vs False
Conflict means contradictory or unresolved support exists.
False is a legal/factual conclusion and may not be inferred automatically.

## Rule
CASECORE organizes what is supported, unsupported, disputed, conflicting, or missing. It does not “construct truth.”
