# EVID 622 — Truth of Facts Recited in Written Instrument

## Text
The facts recited in a written instrument are conclusively presumed to be true as between the parties thereto, or their successors in interest; but this rule does not apply to the recital of a consideration.

## Why it Matters
This section can lock in factual recitals in written instruments as conclusively true between the parties and their successors in interest, with an important exception for recital of consideration.

## War Room Use
- Powerful in contract and document-heavy disputes
- Supports use of written recitals to foreclose contrary factual positions
- Useful when aligning documentary evidence to claims, burdens, and remedies

## Related CACI
- Potentially important in contract, fraud, ownership, and document-authorship disputes depending on the facts

## Notes
The exception for recital of consideration matters and should not be overlooked.
