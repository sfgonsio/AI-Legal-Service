# EVID 352 — Discretion to Exclude Evidence

## Text
The court in its discretion may exclude evidence if its probative value is substantially outweighed by the probability that its admission will (a) necessitate undue consumption of time or (b) create substantial danger of undue prejudice, of confusing the issues, or of misleading the jury.

## Notes
- Core balancing test for admissibility.
- Even relevant evidence can be excluded.

## Platform Relevance
- Critical for admissibility risk scoring.
- Used to flag:
  - prejudicial evidence
  - confusing or misleading content
  - low-value/high-risk artifacts
