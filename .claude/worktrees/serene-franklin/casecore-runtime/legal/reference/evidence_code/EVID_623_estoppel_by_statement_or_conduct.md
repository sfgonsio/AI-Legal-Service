# EVID 623 — Estoppel by Statement or Conduct

## Text
Whenever a party has, by his own statement or conduct, intentionally and deliberately led another to believe a particular thing true and to act upon such belief, he is not, in any litigation arising out of such statement or conduct, permitted to contradict it.

## Why it Matters
This section creates a strong estoppel rule that can prevent a party from contradicting the factual position they intentionally and deliberately induced another to rely upon.

## War Room Use
- High-value authority in reliance and contradiction disputes
- Useful where a party’s prior statement or conduct induced action
- Supports strategy for locking admissions and preventing reversal of position

## Related CACI
- Potentially relevant to reliance-based claims and defenses, including fraud and estoppel-related theories

## Notes
This can be strategically powerful where the record shows intentional inducement and action in reliance.
